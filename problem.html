UNEMPLOYMENT IN TURKEY

The quantity of individuals enrolled as jobless rose to 4.67 million in the three months to February, a flood of more than one million from a year sooner, information from the Turkish Statistics Institute appeared. Non-farming joblessness remained at 16.8 percent in a similar period, the information appeared, hopping from 15.6 percent in the November-January period. In February, the legislature and Union of Chambers and Commodity Exchanges propelled a crusade to help work, which they said will give occupations 2.5 million unemployment. Be that as it may, Turkish joblessness was required to keep ascending in the months ahead and burden private utilization and venture, appraisals office Moody's said in March.Tatha Ghose, senior emerging markets economist at Commerzbank, said: “The labor market is a lagging indicator and we all know Turkey is in a recession, and until that changes, unemployment is expected to go up.”

The main reasons for the unemployment problem in Turkey are as follows:The high young population causes rapid participation in the labor force.Current job opportunities for young people are far from meeting their labor supply. Turkey are behind flexible employment-based employment opportunities, such as part-time jobs, compared to western countries and some developing countries. There is a deep skill mismatch between job seekers and employers in the labor market due to problems with the education system.While employers cannot find employees that meet the criteria they want, hundreds of thousands of people also find it difficult to find jobs in their own fields.

Failure of a low and inadequate economic situation to meet basic physiological requirements such as nutrition and housing can lead to significant physical and mental illnesses. Fear of the future, insecurity in these people; lack of social interest, respect, dignity; the impossibility to prove itself plays a negative role as a socially damaging factor. In addition, conflicts related to family friction caused by low and inadequate economic situations increase the level of anxiety. If this level is high for a long time, it also increases the level of anxiety and causes symptoms and complaints due to difficulty. Living in this section increases fight and anger. It is impossible for young people living in big cities to join and integrate with society and adapt to common social behavior patterns, especially because of youth unemployment. For this reason, it is a natural consequence of these unemployed youth to behave immorally and to be in groups with different religious or ethnic subcultures, and to behave in harmful ways to individuals and the society.Social outcomes of joblessness can likewise remember an expansion for crime percentages, a crumbling in the social structure, an imbalanced movement and an increment in suicide cases. Joblessness prompts a diminishing in pay expected by the individual, an impediment in utilization propensities and social disconnection.According to TUIK data, the rate of those who commit suicide from livelihood is 8%.

As a result, unemployment is not only a social problem, but also a problem that can be the source of other social problems such as disruption of family relations, poverty, crime, suicide. With the causes and consequences of unemployment, which is an important problem of business and social life, it is very important to treat it not only as an economic phenomenon but also as a social-psychological problem.Ignoring the social and psychological consequences of unemployment causes deepening of many problems such as poverty, crime and suicide. It is necessary to understand the importance of social disintegration and deterioration that a situation such as unemployment can create and find solutions before the problems of the unemployed become more chronic.
